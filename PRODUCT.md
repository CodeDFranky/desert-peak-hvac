# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Henderson and greater–Las Vegas homeowners, plus small local business owners
(offices, retail, light industrial), who need HVAC service. They usually arrive
in a moment of friction: an AC that quit in triple-digit summer heat, a furnace
that won't start on a cold morning, or an aging system they no longer trust.
Most land on the page **on a phone**, often outdoors or in an uncomfortable
house, with low patience and high urgency. The job to be done is simple and
pressing: *find a trustworthy local company and reach a real technician fast* —
by calling or submitting a request in as few steps as possible.

## Product Purpose

The marketing homepage for Desert Peak Heating & Cooling, a locally owned HVAC
company serving Henderson since 2009. Its single purpose is **conversion**: turn
a stressed visitor into a phone call or a submitted service request. Everything
else — services, service area, testimonials — exists to build enough trust, fast
enough, that the visitor picks up the phone. Success looks like an obvious,
always-reachable call-to-action and a page that feels like a real, established
local business rather than a generic template.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, direct, locally owned. Three words: **established, reliable,
neighborly**. The voice is plain-spoken and confident — a company that has done
this work in this valley for over a decade and doesn't need to oversell. It is
deliberately **not corporate** (no faceless enterprise polish) and **not
startup** (no playful gradients, no "disruption"). It should evoke the quiet
confidence of a clean, well-maintained work truck: competent, cared-for, ready.

## Anti-references

The interface must NOT look like a SaaS template. Specifically avoid:
- Purple gradients
- Glassmorphism / frosted-glass panels
- Pill buttons everywhere
- Three equal feature cards in a row
- Warm cream backgrounds
- Decorative SVG illustrations
- Anything that reads as a generic AI-generated / SaaS marketing site

## Design Principles

- **Local, not corporate.** Every choice should read like a family business
  that has served Henderson since 2009 — real photos, real credentials, real
  phone number — not a national franchise or a venture-backed app.
- **Urgency without panic.** The summer-heat pain is genuine, so the phone
  number and same-day service must be impossible to miss — but the tone stays
  calm and competent, the reassurance of someone who has fixed this a thousand
  times.
- **Proof over promises.** Lead with evidence of trust — NSCB license #0089421,
  NATE-certified techs, 15+ years local, satisfaction guarantee — rather than
  adjectives. Show the credentials the way a clean truck shows care.
- **Phone-first.** The primary conversion is a call from a distressed mobile
  visitor. The call-to-action is the spine of the page; every section funnels
  back to it.
- **Earned polish.** It should look like a real design studio made it. Craft in
  type, spacing, and hierarchy is how a small local company signals competence —
  the digital equivalent of a well-organized service van.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Navy/white and red/white pairings must clear 4.5:1 for
  body text and 3:1 for large text and UI.
- **Mobile-first**, verified at 375 / 768 / 1280px. Primary audience is on phones
  in the field.
- Tap targets ≥ 44px; the phone CTA is reachable with one thumb.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; no motion is required to understand or use
  the page.
- High legibility for outdoor/bright-sun mobile reading: strong contrast,
  restrained type sizes down to 375px, no thin light-gray text on white.
- Semantic HTML and a logical heading order throughout.
